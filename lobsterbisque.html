Lobster Bisque


To prepare a luxurious Lobster Bisque, start by cooking the lobster meat. Remove the lobster meat from the shells, chop it into bite-sized pieces, and set it aside. Save the shells for making a lobster stock. In a large pot, melt 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter over medium heat. Add 1 finely chopped onion, 2-3 chopped carrots, and 2-3 finely chopped celery stalks. Saut√© the vegetables until they soften and the onion turns translucent. Stir in 2 tablespoons of tomato paste and 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour, cooking for a few minutes to remove the raw flour taste. Optional: Add 1/4 cup of brandy or cognac and let it simmer for a minute to enhance the flavor. Pour in 1/4 cup of dry white wine and cook until it reduces by half. Add the lobster shells, 4 cups of chicken or seafood stock, 2-3 bay leaves, fresh or dried thyme leaves, a pinch of cayenne pepper (if desired), and salt and pepper to taste. Simmer for about 30-40 minutes, then strain to remove the shells and solids. Return the strained liquid to the pot, add 2 cups of heavy cream, and bring it to a gentle simmer. Finally, add the chopped lobster meat and cook for a few minutes until it's heated through. Adjust the seasoning, and your delectable lobster bisque is ready to serve. Enjoy this indulgent soup with a touch of elegance!
