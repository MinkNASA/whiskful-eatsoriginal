French Fries


To make crispy and delicious French fries at home, start by peeling and cutting russet or Idaho potatoes into uniform, thin strips. Rinse the potato strips in cold water to remove excess starch, then pat them dry with a clean kitchen towel. In a deep pot or a deep-fryer, heat vegetable oil to around 350°F (175°C). Carefully add the potato strips in small batches, ensuring they don't overcrowd the pot, and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy, which usually takes about 3-4 minutes per batch. Use a slotted spoon to remove the fries from the hot oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil. While they're still hot, sprinkle the French fries with salt to taste. Serve immediately for the best taste and texture. Enjoy your homemade French fries with your favorite dipping sauces or seasonings!
