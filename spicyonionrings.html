Spicy Onion Rings


To make crispy and Spicy Onion Rings, begin by preparing 2-3 large sweet onions, such as Vidalia or Walla Walla, by slicing them into rings and separating the layers. In a bowl, pour approximately 2 cups of buttermilk and submerge the onion rings, allowing them to soak for about 30 minutes. In another bowl, combine 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1/2 to 1 cup of cornmeal (for added crunch, if desired), 1-2 teaspoons of paprika, 1/2 to 1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (adjust to your preferred level of spiciness), and a pinch of salt and pepper. Heat enough vegetable oil for deep frying in a heavy-bottomed pot or fryer to 350-375Â°F (175-190Â°C). Remove the onion rings from the buttermilk, allowing any excess to drip off, and coat them thoroughly in the spicy flour mixture. Carefully place the coated onion rings into the hot oil in batches, ensuring not to overcrowd the pot. Fry until they turn golden brown and crispy, typically for about 2-3 minutes per batch. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the Spicy Onion Rings to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil. Serve your delicious onion rings immediately as an appetizer or side dish, and enjoy the crispy, spicy goodness!
