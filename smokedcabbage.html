Smoked Cabbage


To prepare Smoked Cabbage, start by removing any damaged outer leaves from a large head of green cabbage, leaving the core intact. Carefully make a deep incision in the center of the cabbage, creating a crater-like pocket for the butter and seasonings. Fill this pocket with approximately 1/2 to 1 cup of butter, then season the cabbage generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Next, wrap the cabbage tightly in aluminum foil to create a sealed packet. Preheat your smoker or grill to a low to medium-low temperature, around 225-250°F (107-121°C). Place the foil-wrapped cabbage on the grill grates, close the lid, and let it smoke for about 2-3 hours. The cooking time may vary depending on the size and heat of your smoker or grill, so check the cabbage's tenderness with a fork. When it's fork-tender and has a delightful smoky flavor, it's ready to be served. Carefully unwrap the foil, slice the smoked cabbage into wedges, and enjoy the unique smoky and buttery goodness as a side dish for your meal.
