Churros


To create scrumptious churros at home, start by heating 1 cup of water and 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter in a saucepan over medium heat until it comes to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and add 1 tablespoon of granulated sugar and a pinch of salt. Stir in 1 cup of all-purpose flour until you have a smooth dough that pulls away from the sides of the pan. Remove it from the heat and let it cool for a few minutes before adding 2 large eggs, one at a time, and stirring until fully incorporated. If desired, add 1/2 to 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract for flavor. Transfer the dough to a piping bag fitted with a star tip. In a separate pot or deep fryer, heat vegetable oil to 350°F (175°C). Carefully pipe 4-6 inch strips of dough into the hot oil, using scissors or a knife to cut them. Fry until they turn golden brown and crisp, which usually takes about 2-4 minutes. Remove the churros from the oil and drain on paper towels. Roll them in granulated sugar while still warm. Serve your homemade churros with chocolate sauce, caramel, or your favorite dipping sauce. Enjoy the irresistible taste of freshly made churros!
